# SliceLogger
SliceLogger is a full-featured ham radio logging program engineered to integrate with FlexRadio SDRs that use the SmartSDR API, such as the Aurora, FLEX-8000, and FLEX-6000 series radios

It's been a dream of mine to create a logging program that fully integrates with my FLEX-6700, Flex-8600M, Aurora AU-520, and PGXL. Finally, with a little help from my good buddy Claude, that dream has come to fruition with SliceLogger.

SliceLogger is a full-featured ham radio logging program designed to leverage SmartSDR radio hardware by interfacing directly with a FLEX-6000, FLEX-8000, or Aurora radio.  It is SmartLink and multiFlex-aware, and integrates with the 4O3A PGXL.

A complete overview of everything SliceLogger does is found in the Feature Help file — from rapid manual QSO entry to automatic WSJT-X and FLDigi logging, FlexRadio slice tracking, SmartLink support, and live radio telemetry, LoTW, and eQSL.cc upload and sync, Award tracking, logbook ADIF import, paper QSL card tracking, DXCC entity tracking, space weather, the interactive Grid Map of worked squares, and the Grayline / Spots Globe with real-time 3-D day/night shading and live DX spot visualization.

<strong>Getting Started:</strong> After downloading and installing the application on your Windows 10 or Windows 11 PC, navigate to the <i>Help->Features</i> menu selection to familiarize yourself with SliceLogger features.  

Then select <i>Help->Getting Started</i>, which will guide you through setting up an Operator (that would be you!) and connecting to a radio.  

Next, take a look at the <i>Help->Radio Setup</i>, which covers how to discover your radio and log into a SmartLink account to use remote radios.  As a SmartSDR user, the concepts should be second nature, but this will familiarize you  with using SliceLogger.

Then check out the <i>Help->Entering a QSO</i>, and you should be on your way!
